How Serious is Mental Health in Australia?

Mental illness is a general term that refers to a group of illnesses that affects the brain.
It is a health problem that signifiacntly affects how a person feels, think, behaves and interacts with other people.
People with mental health conditions often also experience stigma, discrimination and human rights violations.

Key Mental Health issues across different states in Australia

The choropleth map highlights the prevalence of key mental health issues, including:

  • Affective disorders
  • Anxiety disorders
  • Substance use disorders

across different regions in Australia.

Distinct regional variations emerge, with certain areas showing disproportionately high rates of anxiety and affective disorders, indicating potential socio-economic or environmental factors at play.

The map suggests that support systems may need to be tailored to specific regions where these mental health challenges are most severe.

Psychological Distress experienced across genders

What is psychological distress?
Measures of psychological distress may include

  • Nervousness,
  • Agitation,
  • Psychological fatigue and
  • Depression.

This distress can result in having negative views of the environment, others, and oneself.

This radial visualization offers a deep dive into the psychological distress experienced by different genders. It reveals that:

  • Non-binary individuals face disproportionately higher levels of distress, particularly in terms of anxiety and affective disorders.
  • Females show significant levels of anxiety.
  • Males report higher substance use disorders.

These insights call for gender-sensitive mental health interventions to address the distinct challenges faced by different gender groups.

Prevalence of Mental Disorders across different age groups and Suicidal ideation

The stacked bar chart shifts focus to the relationship between age and the prevalence of mental disorders, including anxiety, affective, and substance use disorders.
Young adults, especially those aged 16-34, face higher rates of psychological distress, with non-binary individuals again showing the highest prevalence.
This insight underscores how age and gender intersect to create distinct mental health challenges that require a nuanced, age- and gender-sensitive approach to mental health care.

Suicidal ideation

The treemap provides a stark look at the gender differences in suicidal thoughts, plans, and attempts.
Non-binary individuals experience disproportionately high rates of suicidal ideation, with significant numbers also seen among males and females. This highlights the critical need for targeted mental health interventions, particularly for those struggling with severe mental distress across genders.
